ETFs (exchange-traded funds) let you own slices of many companies at once with a single purchase. For most beginner investors, broad-market ETFs like these tend to outperform picking individual stocks over the long term — without the research or stress.

VOO
Vanguard S&P 500
Tracks the 500 biggest US companies. The default "I want to own American business" choice.
 VTI
Total US Stock Market
Owns essentially every public US stock, weighted by size. More diversified than VOO.
 QQQ
NASDAQ 100
100 biggest NASDAQ-listed companies. Tech-heavy — Apple, Microsoft, NVIDIA, etc.
 VT
Total World Stock
Global stocks, US and international combined. Maximum geographic diversification.
 VIG
Dividend Appreciation
Companies with growing dividend payments. Lower yield, more stable than most stock ETFs.
 BND
Total Bond Market
Bonds, not stocks. Lower returns but more predictable. Often part of a balanced portfolio.
